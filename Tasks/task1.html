Elon Musk is a South African-born American entrepreneur and innovator, best known for his roles as CEO and co-founder of SpaceX, CEO and product architect of Tesla Inc., and co-founder of Neuralink and The Boring Company. He is also a pioneer in the development of private space exploration and electric vehicles. Musk was born in 1971 in Pretoria, South Africa. At 12, he created a video game and sold it to a computer magazine. He left South Africa in 1988 to attend Queen’s University in Kingston, Ontario, Canada, and later transferred to the University of Pennsylvania, where he received bachelor’s degrees in physics and economics. In 1995, he moved to California to attend graduate school in physics at Stanford University.
